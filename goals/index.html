Disclaimer: I've a very loose definition of the word 'goal'.. and apparently, a very loose definition of what achieving those goals looks like, too.

I'm hoping to write up a lovely and coherent blog post about the deeper through process behind these goals, but for now, I'll just leave you with a general introduction.

Below, you'll find a hundred 'goals', or something along those lines. Im actually omitting a very big cheat - I didn't think up a hundred goals. Not even close - I think there's actually something like.. well, I'll count a bit later. As I mentioned before, I've been quite loose with these goals. There's eight categories, in no particular order:

  • Tech - 10 goals
  • Organisation - 10 goals
  • Fitness - 10 goals
  • Reading - 10 goals
  • Travel - 10 goals
  • Wildcards - 10 goals
  • Skills* - this is a double category, with 20 goals

In the true spirit of not wanting to limit myself, and having discovered that I am horrible at goal setting, a lot of the goals are either very vibes-based or very vague, and very much up to my own personal interpretation of whether I've achieved something. This allows me to factor in any unforseen skills that I may have had no prior knowledge that I'd learn, but can also allow for the encouragement to hunt out new things and move further out of my comfort zone and into the unknown.

In future, I will be coding a cute little progress bar here somewhere, and will also be digging out my Javascript knowledge to tidy this page up, but for now..

As a child, I loved reading. My weekends were spent at the library, my reading journal was always full, and I had hundreds of unfinished novel drafts on my home computer that were usually self-inserts about the stables I'd run that bore a striking similarity to the Heartland series that I was obsessed with. I still do love reading, but I find that I don't make enough time for it, and I don't put enough effort into reading diverse works. All too often I find myself sitting on my phone before bed, doomscrolling, giving myself thumb problems and ending the day comparing myself to a random stranger on the internet when actually, I could be reading? Even though fantasy/romantasy is my favourite, I also want to make sure I'm reading non-fiction too. Five of each can't be that hard?
